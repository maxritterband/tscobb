T. S. Cobb

T. S. Cobb spent twenty-five years in finance — analyzing companies and eventually running a hedge fund — before leaving to write. His essays and his first book, For What It's Worth: In Search of a Good Life, explore the things that can't be measured, planned, or forced into being. He holds degrees in history and theology and writes from a small town in Virginia.
